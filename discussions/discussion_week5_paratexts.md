## Prompt

Genette defines the paratext as a “threshold” that mediates between text and reader — not simply a frame, but a zone of transaction that shapes reception and interpretation.

Walsh extends this framework to comics, arguing that elements often dismissed as peripheral (advertisements, fan mail, indicia, documentary materials, notes, dedications, etc.) are constitutive of the comic book document.

Choose **one specific comic book paratext** and analyze it using Genette’s framework.

You might consider:

*   A cover (price, Comics Code seal, creator credits, hype copy)
*   A splash page or indicia
*   An advertisement (including those using comics form)
*   A fan letter page
*   A dedication or epigraph
*   A documentary insert (map, diagram, pin-up, timeline)
*   A note referencing prior continuity
*   An interview or creator statement (epitext)

* * *

## In your response:

1.  **Identify the paratext clearly** (issue title, issue number, publisher, date).
2.  Classify it:
    *   Peritext or epitext?
    *   Public or private?
    *   Authorial, publisher, or allographic?
3.  Analyze its **function**. What does it do?
    *   Frame interpretation?
    *   Establish authority?
    *   Signal genre?
    *   Build continuity?
    *   Create reader community?
    *   Market the comic?
4.  Reflect briefly on this question:  
    **If this paratext were removed, how would the reading experience change?**
