## Prompt

This week’s readings introduce the idea of treating **“text as data.”** Computational text analysis allows researchers to analyze large collections of texts in order to identify patterns that would be difficult to see through traditional close reading alone. At the same time, the readings emphasize that these methods do not replace interpretation; rather, they must be used carefully and critically. Automated analysis can reveal patterns across large corpora, but its results still require human interpretation and validation.

Comic-book **letter columns** and **letterhacks** provide a rich archive of fan discourse. Taken together across decades and titles, these letters form a large body of text that could potentially be analyzed using computational methods. They document how fans discuss stories, creators, characters, and each other, offering insight into the development of comics fandom over time.

Choose **one key idea, tension, or claim** from the readings that you find especially productive for thinking about computational approaches to comics and fandom.

In your response:

1. Briefly explain how the author frames this issue.  
2. Consider how computational text analysis might be applied to a corpus of comic-book fan mail or other fan discourse.

You might consider questions such as:

- What kinds of questions about comics fandom might computational text analysis help answer?  
- What patterns might emerge if we analyzed hundreds or thousands of fan letters across decades?  
- What kinds of **limitations or biases** might affect computational analysis of fan mail (for example, editorial selection of letters, OCR errors, or changing fan language)?  
- How might computational analysis complement more traditional humanities methods such as close reading or historical interpretation?  
- What might we learn about **participatory culture** if we treated fan letters as a dataset rather than only as individual texts?

You may also reflect briefly on how computational methods might change how scholars study comics fandom more broadly.
